- **Basic KVM VPS** — affordable general-purpose servers across multiple global locations, fine for most non-China-traffic workloads.
- **E-Commerce VPS** — premium China-bound routing (CN2 GIA / CTGNet / CMIN2 / Unicom Premium) without the premium price tag, available in Los Angeles and Osaka.

Understanding this four-tier structure is the single most important thing before you buy. A lot of buyers pick a plan by RAM size and end up overpaying for routing they don't need, or underpaying and getting congestion during China peak hours.

- **If you don't care about China**: pick the Basic KVM VPS tier. The 20G plan at $49.99/year is fine for a personal VPN, a small site, or a learning sandbox. The 80G plan at $19.99/month is the sensible upgrade when you outgrow it.

## First Things To Do After Buying (Before You Put Anything Important On The Server)

Once the VPS is up, three quick steps save a lot of grief later:

1. **Run a fresh OS reinstall from KiwiVM.** Even though the VPS ships with an OS installed, doing a clean reinstall from the KiwiVM templates guarantees you're starting from a known-good state, not whatever snapshot the provisioning system happened to use. Takes about two minutes.
2. **Change the SSH password and disable root login if you're going to expose SSH.** BandwagonHost randomizes the port, which helps, but adding a non-root user with sudo and disabling root SSH login is the standard hygiene move.
3. **Take a snapshot.** KiwiVM snapshots are free and can be cloned between VPSes. Taking one right after initial setup means you can always roll back to a clean state without re-provisioning.

For Dubai VPS specifically, KiwiVM also runs **automatic backups every few days** at no extra cost — a feature that's normally a paid add-on with most providers.

## Common Buying Mistakes To Avoid

A handful of patterns show up often enough in user discussions that they're worth flagging:

- **Picking a plan by RAM size alone.** A 4 GB Basic KVM plan and a 4 GB E-Commerce plan are not the same product. The Basic plan is cheaper per GB of RAM because the routing is cheaper. If your traffic goes to China, the Basic plan will feel slower during peak hours even though the spec sheet looks identical.
- **Buying the cheapest limited-edition plan and assuming it's always available.** The $29.99/year Black Friday plans and $49.99/year CN2 GIA-E limited editions are real and genuinely good value, but stock is intermittent. Have a regular plan as a fallback rather than waiting indefinitely for a restock.
- **Forgetting that limited plans are sometimes non-migratable.** Some promotional plans lock you to a specific datacenter. Read the plan's "Migration" note before assuming you can move it later.
- **Skipping the coupon.** A 6% recurring discount doesn't sound like much, but over a three-year lifespan on a mid-tier plan it's a meaningful chunk of money back in your pocket.
- **Ignoring renewal pricing.** A few limited-edition plans renew at a higher price than the first-year promo. The first payment and renewal price aren't always identical — confirm both at checkout.
